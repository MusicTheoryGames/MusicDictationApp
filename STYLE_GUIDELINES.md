# CLASSICAL STYLE GUIDELINES FOR MELODIC DICTATION
## Mozart, Haydn, and Beethoven Melodic Practices

### OVERVIEW
All melodies in this application must follow Classical period compositional practices (approximately 1750-1820), specifically emulating the melodic style of Mozart, Haydn, and Beethoven. These guidelines ensure educational authenticity and musical coherence.

---

## 1. MELODIC MOTION PRINCIPLES

### Stepwise Motion Priority
- **Minimum 60% stepwise motion** (movement by 2nd)
- **Ideal 70-80% stepwise motion** for most phrases
- Steps should predominate over leaps in Classical style

### Leap Guidelines
- **Small leaps (3rd-5th)**: Acceptable and common
- **Large leaps (6th-octave)**: Use sparingly, require stepwise resolution
- **Intervals larger than octave**: Forbidden
- **Tritones**: Avoid in melodic motion (use only in harmonic context)

### Leap Resolution Rules
- **Ascending leap**: Follow with stepwise descent when possible
- **Descending leap**: Follow with stepwise ascent when possible
- **Large leaps**: Must resolve by step in opposite direction

## 2. RHYTHM PATTERNS BY TIME SIGNATURE

### 4/4 Time (Common Time)
**Simple Rhythms:**
- Whole notes, half notes, quarter notes
- Basic quarter + quarter combinations
- Half note + two quarters

**Complex Rhythms:**
- Eighth note pairs (always grouped)
- Dotted quarter + eighth combinations
- Mixed quarter and eighth patterns
- Avoid syncopation in Easy level

### 3/4 Time (Waltz Time)
**Simple Rhythms:**
- Dotted half notes (full measure)
- Half note + quarter
- Three quarter notes

**Complex Rhythms:**
- Quarter + eighth pairs
- Dotted quarter + eighth + quarter
- Mixed subdivision patterns

### 6/8 Time (Compound Duple)
**Simple Rhythms:**
- Two dotted quarters
- Dotted quarter + quarter + eighth
- Quarter + eighth + dotted quarter

**Complex Rhythms:**
- Six eighth notes
- Mixed dotted quarter and eighth patterns
- Compound groupings (3+3)

### 9/8 Time (Compound Triple)
**Simple Rhythms:**
- Three dotted quarters
- Dotted quarter combinations with quarters/eighths

**Complex Rhythms:**
- Mixed compound groupings (3+3+3)
- Dotted quarters with eighth subdivisions

## 3. PHRASE STRUCTURE AND CONTOUR

### 2-Measure Phrases
- **Simple arch**: Start middle → rise → descend
- **Linear motion**: Stepwise ascent or descent
- **Return pattern**: Away from tonic → return to tonic

### 4-Measure Phrases
- **Classical curve**: Start middle → gradual rise → peak at measure 3 → descend to cadence
- **Peak placement**: Typically measure 2-3 (avoid measure 1 or 4)
- **Range**: 5-12 semitones typical

### 8-Measure Periods
- **Two-part structure**: Question (measures 1-4) + Answer (measures 5-8)
- **Mid-point cadence**: Half cadence or similar pause at measure 4
- **Final cadence**: Authentic cadence at measure 8
- **Climax placement**: Typically measures 5-6 (60-75% through)
- **Range**: 8-19 semitones acceptable

## 4. TONAL REQUIREMENTS

### C Major
- **Scale degrees**: C-D-E-F-G-A-B-C
- **No accidentals** in natural major
- **Cadential patterns**: Emphasize 7-1, 2-1, 5-1 motion
- **Avoid**: Chromatic alterations (save for advanced levels)

### A Minor
- **Natural minor**: A-B-C-D-E-F-G-A
- **Harmonic minor**: A-B-C-D-E-F-G#-A (raised 7th)
- **Melodic minor**: A-B-C-D-E-F#-G#-A ascending (use sparingly)
- **Cadential patterns**: Emphasize G#-A (leading tone resolution)

---

## 5. CADENTIAL PATTERNS

### Half Cadences (for measure 4 in 8-measure examples)
- **Melodic motion to 5**: End on G (in C major) or E (in A minor)
- **Approach patterns**: 
  - 4-5 (F-G or D-E)
  - 2-5 (D-G or B-E)
  - 6-5 (A-G or F-E)

### Authentic Cadences (for final measures)
- **Melodic motion to 1**: End on C (in C major) or A (in A minor)
- **Approach patterns**:
  - 7-1 (B-C or G#-A) - most common
  - 2-1 (D-C or B-A) - also strong
  - 5-1 (G-C or E-A) - direct resolution

## 6. OPTION VARIATION STRATEGIES

All 6 options must be similar but distinct. Use these variation techniques:

### Single Note Changes
- **Step substitution**: Change one note by step (C→D or C→B)
- **Direction change**: C-D-E → C-B-A (same intervals, opposite direction)
- **Register shift**: Move phrase up/down an octave (within comfortable range)

### Rhythmic Variations
- **Subdivision**: Quarter note → two eighths
- **Combination**: Two eighths → quarter note
- **Dot addition/removal**: Quarter → dotted quarter + eighth

### Intervallic Variations
- **Leap size**: 3rd → 4th → 5th (similar motion, different size)
- **Leap direction**: Ascending → descending (same size, opposite direction)

### Ornamental Variations
- **Passing tones**: Add stepwise connection between leaps
- **Neighbor tones**: Add upper/lower neighbor figures
- **Keep minimal**: Only 1-2 ornamental changes per option

## 7. NOTE RANGE GUIDELINES

### Treble Clef
- **C Major**: C4 to G5 comfortable range
- **A Minor**: A3 to E5 comfortable range
- **Avoid**: Extreme high notes (above A5) or low notes (below G3)

### Bass Clef
- **C Major**: C3 to G4 comfortable range  
- **A Minor**: A2 to E4 comfortable range
- **Avoid**: Extreme high notes (above A4) or low notes (below F2)

---

## 8. FORBIDDEN PRACTICES

### Melodic
- ❌ Augmented or diminished intervals (except in minor scales)
- ❌ Chromatic alterations outside key signature
- ❌ Tritone leaps
- ❌ Intervals larger than an octave
- ❌ More than two consecutive leaps in same direction

### Rhythmic
- ❌ Syncopation (save for advanced levels)
- ❌ Complex subdivisions (16th notes, etc.)
- ❌ Cross-rhythm patterns
- ❌ Irregular groupings

### Structural
- ❌ Unclear phrase boundaries
- ❌ Missing or incorrect cadences (8-measure examples)
- ❌ Excessive repetition of same note
- ❌ Static melodic motion (no clear direction)
